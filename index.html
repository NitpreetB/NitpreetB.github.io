About Me  

Hi, I am Nitpreet Bamra, a Mechatronics Engineering student at the University of Waterloo. The interconnectivity between software development and mechanical design, alongside their real-world applications, motivated my love for robotics and computing and inspired me to join the Mechatronics program. Over the years, I have developed a solid foundational understanding of Machine Learning (ML), Computer Vision (CV) and Artificial intelligence (AI) concepts through the likes of my previous work terms and personal projects.

Previoius Work Experience  

My latest CO-OP opportunity was working for Arcturus Networks as an Embedded Machine Vision Developer. I developed highly advanced Machine Vision and Machine Learning algorithms for this job while leveraging current open-source frameworks like PyTorch and Google Cloud Platform. I tested, trained and optimized yolov5 models for multiple object tracking, ranking the global leader in detection accuracy. Previously, I also worked for the University of Waterloo's Vision and Image Processing lab as a member of the Blue Lion Labs teams. For Blue Lion Labs, I worked as a machine learning research engineer, where I spent my time researching and implementing Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) machine learning models. Specifically, I conducted an empirical experiment and wrote a research report about the feasibility of using GANs to generate high-resolution and photorealistic synthetic phytoplankton images given a small number of training images.
